A disability refers to a condition of the body or mind that creates challenges for individuals when performing certain activities and interacting with the world. People with disabilities comprise a diverse group with varying needs. Not all disabilities are immediately visible, and two individuals with the same disability may experience it differently.

Disability can be:

  • Developmental conditions that appear in childhood, such as autism spectrum disorder and ADHD.
  • Intermittent disabilities like certain forms of multiple sclerosis.
  • Progressive disabilities like muscular dystrophy.
  • Static disabilities like limb loss.
  • Disabilities resulting from long-standing conditions such as diabetes, which can lead to complications like vision loss, nerve damage, or limb loss.
